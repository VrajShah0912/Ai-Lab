The Taj Mahal is one of the most iconic monuments in the world. 
It is located in Agra, India. 
The monument was commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. 
Construction began in 1632 and took about 22 years to complete. 
The Taj Mahal is made primarily of white marble. 
It is a symbol of eternal love and devotion. 
The structure is a blend of Islamic, Persian, and Indian architectural styles. 
The central dome is flanked by four minarets at the corners. 
Visitors from all over the world come to see its beauty. 
It is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. 
The monument is situated on the banks of the Yamuna River. 
Intricate carvings and precious stones adorn its walls. 
The gardens surrounding the Taj Mahal are laid out in a classic Mughal style. 
During sunrise and sunset, the Taj Mahal glows with different hues. 
It was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983. 
Legends say that Shah Jahan intended to build a black marble Taj Mahal across the river. 
Every year, millions of tourists visit Agra to see this masterpiece. 
Night viewing is allowed on five days a month, including the full moon. 
The Taj Mahal faces threats from pollution and environmental factors. 
Efforts are being made to preserve its pristine beauty. 
It remains a timeless testament to architectural brilliance and human emotion.
