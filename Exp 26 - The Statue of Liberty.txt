The Statue of Liberty is a universal symbol of freedom and democracy. 
It is located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor. 
The statue was a gift from France to the United States in 1886. 
Designed by Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi, it represents Libertas, the Roman goddess of liberty. 
The statue holds a torch above her head with her right hand. 
In her left hand, she carries a tabula ansata inscribed with the date of American independence. 
The full height from the ground to the tip of the torch is 305 feet. 
It was constructed using sheets of hammered copper. 
The internal structure was engineered by Gustave Eiffel. 
Over the years, the statue has undergone several restorations.
